Cerebral Horror Game

Spring 2015/Director: Berk Ekmekci

The entire game concept is built around the player experience; an immersive gameplay instilling a subtle sense of dread and uneasiness will lie at the core of the project, and the remaining aspects of the game will follow through as necessary to achieve this goal. This game will focus more on aesthetic, feel, and interactive experience than implementation of target gameplay mechanics.

Rainy Days

Spring 2015/Director: Emily Costigan

When walking home one rainy day, the player character jumps into a puddle to play, and falls all the way through to a strange, new world. The puddle is blocked behind them by dark clouds that cover this new world in rainfall. Equipped with only their umbrella, the player must solve puzzles and navigate through levels to clear the sky and get home, or else be lost in this watery world forever. The game combines puzzle and platforming elements where the umbrella can be used in different ways depending on the context of the situation.

Road to Victory

Spring 2015/Director: Henry Conklin

The Road to Victory is a long one, especially when you can only build roads. I, and countless others, are terrible at real-time strategy games. They require a level of speed and planning simply unavailable to a majority of the population. Most real-time strategy games require you to construct somewhere in the range of 10-20 buildings, each generally with several upgrades, and each producing one of many units or several resources. In Road to Victory, there is only one building, roads, and only one resource, people, who also serve as your only unit. As you build your magnificent highway system, your citizens will build new houses to hold more citizens allowing you to build more roads and command larger armies.

Wonder Games

Spring 2015/Director: Brittany Crow

The Wonder Games will be an engaging, educational game modeled after the Hunger Games.The target audience will be middle-schoolers, with three levels corresponding to 6th, 7th, 8th grade and a challenge level with trivia content. Players will be able to select their character, level and subject pool and then embark on a mission to knock the other competitors out of the arena by correctly answering questions. Competitors will be computer simulated and ask questions based on their specific subject assignment. Random events that can diminish or replenish health add an element of surprise to the game while the mobility of competitors make the game more challenging.
